# Cold Outreach Sequence — Reference Guide

## Full Sequence Example — SaaS to Startup Founder

**Context:** Selling a developer tool to startup CTOs. Credibility: 500 users, Y Combinator companies use it.

### Email 1 (Day 1)
```
Subject: saw you're scaling the eng team

Hey Sarah,

Noticed [Company] just posted 3 backend roles — scaling fast.

[Tool] helps engineering teams [outcome] without [pain]. 500 teams use it, including a few YC companies.

Worth 15 minutes this week?

Tom
```

### Email 2 (Day 3)
```
Subject: Re: saw you're scaling the eng team

Hey Sarah,

Bumping this in case it got buried during the hiring chaos.

Tom
```

### Email 3 (Day 5)
```
Subject: Re: saw you're scaling the eng team

Hey Sarah,

Put together a quick demo of how [Company similar to theirs] set this up in an afternoon: [link]

No ask — just thought it might be useful given where you're at.

Tom
```

### Email 4 (Day 8)
```
Subject: Re: saw you're scaling the eng team

Hey Sarah,

[Similar Company] cut their [painful process] from 2 days to 20 minutes using [Tool]. They were at roughly your stage when they switched.

Happy to show you the same setup — 15 minutes?

Tom
```

### Email 5 (Day 12)
```
Subject: Re: saw you're scaling the eng team

Hey Sarah,

Last note on this — if the timing's off, completely understand. Happy to revisit whenever it makes sense.

Tom
```

## Full Sequence Example — Partnership Outreach

**Context:** Indie builder reaching out to a newsletter writer about a potential sponsorship or collab.

### Email 1 (Day 1)
```
Subject: [Newsletter Name] + [Product] collab?

Hey [Name],

Been reading [Newsletter] for 6 months — your piece on [specific topic] was the best thing I read that week.

Built [Product] for [audience] — [one sentence on what it does]. 2,000 users, all developers.

Worth a quick chat about whether there's a fit?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 (Day 3)
```
Subject: Re: [Newsletter Name] + [Product] collab?

Hey [Name],

Just in case this got lost — totally understand if it's not a fit.

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 (Day 6)
```
Subject: Re: [Newsletter Name] + [Product] collab?

Hey [Name],

Wrote up how [Product] works for your audience specifically: [link to one-pager or landing page].

No pressure — just made it easier to evaluate if it is a fit.

[Your name]
```

## Subject Line A/B Variations

For the same outreach, test these subject line styles:

| Style | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Observation | "saw you're hiring backend engineers" |
| Direct | "quick question about [their product]" |
| Referral | "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out" |
| Compliment specific | "your take on [topic] in [publication]" |
| Curiosity gap | "something I noticed about [their product]" |

Never test: generic ("following up"), spammy ("RE: RE: RE:"), misleading ("your account").

---

## Research Hooks That Work

Before writing Email 1, find one of these:

| Source | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| LinkedIn | Recent post, job change, company milestone |
| Twitter/X | Recent tweet about a problem your product solves |
| Product Hunt | Recent launch, comment they left |
| Crunchbase | Recent funding round ("congrats on the Series A") |
| Their newsletter/blog | Specific piece they wrote, topic they care about |
| Their job postings | What roles they're hiring tells you what they're building |
| Press mentions | Award, feature, quote in an article |

The hook must be:
- Specific (not "I love your work")
- Recent (last 30-60 days)
- Relevant (connected to why you're reaching out)

## Send Time Optimization

Best times to send (based on reply rate data):
- **Day of week:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Time:** 8-9am or 2-3pm recipient's timezone
- **Avoid:** Monday morning (inbox chaos), Friday afternoon (wind-down mode)

For international recipients: always convert to their timezone, not yours.

---

## When to Skip a Touch

Skip Email 4 (social proof) if:
- You don't have real, nameable clients yet
- Your proof is vague ("thousands of users")
- The recipient would know the claim is inflated

A missing Email 4 is better than a fake one. Go straight from Email 3 to Email 5.
